Movements 1–4

🧘 Opening Position · 開始 · Before Every Session

Feet together, arms at sides. Step left foot to shoulder width. Center weight. Bring hands to lower dantian (one hand on top of the other). Close eyes. Pause and breathe naturally for approximately 30 seconds to calm body and mind.

1

Holding Up the Heavens

雙手托天理三焦
Shuāng Shǒu Tuō Tiān Lǐ Sān Jiāo
Release hands from lower dantian and relax arms down. Clasp hands with palms facing upward. Inhale — slowly lift to center of chest. Rotate hands outward, then push upward toward the sky. Exhale — release hands and lower them to each side with palms downward, returning to center in front of lower dantian. This completes one cycle. Repeat for a total of 6 cycles.
↑ Inhale: lift clasped hands to chest ↓ Exhale: push to sky → lower to dantian 🫁 Triple Warmer (San Jiao) · Stretches spine ⚙ Element: Metal
2

Draw the Bow to Shoot the Eagle

左右開弓似射雕
Zuǒ Yòu Kāi Gōng Sì Shè Diāo
Raise arms in front and cross left forearm in front of right directly in front of chest, palms facing you. Step left foot to the left side (twice your shoulder width), toes pointing forward. Inhale — lower into horse stance; pull right hand back to a fist in front of right shoulder while simultaneously pushing left hand in Southern Palm* to the left. Follow your left hand with head/eyes. Exhale — step back to shoulder width, lower both arms to lower dantian level with palms open. Alternate sides. Total: 6 cycles.

*Southern Palm: thumb, 3rd, 4th, and 5th fingers curl inward like a tiger claw; index finger remains fully extended.
↑ Inhale: horse stance + draw bow ↓ Exhale: step back to shoulder width 🫁 Lung + Large Intestine · Opens chest ⚙ Element: Metal
3

Separating Heaven and Earth

調理脾胃須單臂
Tiáo Lǐ Pí Wèi Xū Dān Bì
Feet shoulder width, hands relaxed at lower dantian level (palms up). Inhale — raise both arms in front of torso with palms up; stop at center of chest. Rotate left palm outward until it faces upward. Exhale — push left hand toward sky with palm up while simultaneously pushing right hand toward ground with palm down. Inhale — lower left hand in front while raising right back to center of chest (both palms up). Exhale — repeat with right hand up, left hand down. Total: 6 cycles.
↑ Inhale: both hands to chest center ↓ Exhale: separate — one sky, one earth 🫁 Spleen + Stomach · Massages digestion ⚙ Element: Earth
4

Wise Owl Gazes Backwards

五勞七傷向後瞧
Wǔ Láo Qī Shāng Xiàng Hòu Qiáo
Arms relaxed at both sides, palms facing ground. Inhale — rotate both arms from the shoulder until both palms turn outward as far as possible with thumbs pointing backward as far as possible. Simultaneously rotate head to the left, looking across the horizon as far left as possible (even turn eyes to the side). Exhale — turn head back to center while rotating both arms back to starting position, palms down. Repeat on right side for second cycle. Total: 6 cycles alternating.
↑ Inhale: rotate arms out + turn head ↓ Exhale: return head and arms to center 🫁 Heart + Cervical vessels · Reduces stress ⚙ Element: Fire

Movements 5–8
5

Sway the Head and Shake the Tail

搖頭擺尾去心火
Yáo Tóu Bǎi Wěi Qù Xīn Huǒ
Step left foot to left side, sit in a horse stance with body tall and toes pointing forward. Place each hand on inner thigh near the knee (fingers on inside of thigh). Inhale — rotate torso in a circle starting to the right and moving left all the way around. Circle a second time, then Exhale — twist to the left side and look behind you while leaning over your left thigh with your right leg extending behind you. Inhale — swing back to the right side, return to horse stance. Perform one more swing on the right side for the second cycle. Total: 6 cycles. Return to standing with feet shoulder width.
↑ Inhale: circle torso in horse stance ↓ Exhale: twist and look behind, lean over thigh 🫁 Heart · Reduces Heart Fire (君火) ⚙ Element: Fire
6

Hold the Feet to Strengthen the Kidneys

兩手把住足根搖
Liǎng Shǒu Bǎ Zhù Zú Gēn Yáo
Clasp hands together in front of lower dantian. Inhale — raise hands palms up to chest height. Rotate palms forward, push to front, Exhale. Inhale — raise arms above your head. Release hands and rotate to palms downward. Pull down to chest height while Exhaling. Inhale — rotate palms upward, pull hands along chest at solar plexus level until reaching your back at the same level. Exhale — slide hands down your back, leading with fingers; slide along back, buttocks, hamstrings, lower legs, outside edge of feet, and extend arms to front as far as possible with palms facing each other. Inhale — lift torso with arms extended until you return to standing. Rotate palms down, lower to chest level. Total: 6 cycles.
↑ Inhale: raise, then lift torso back to stand ↓ Exhale: forward bend, slide hands full length 🫁 Kidney + Urinary Bladder · Strengthens lumbar ⚙ Element: Water
7

Clench the Fists and Gaze Fiercely

攥拳怒目增氣力
Zuàn Quán Nù Mù Zēng Qì Lì
Step left foot to left side, sit in horse stance with body tall and toes forward. Make two fists and place them at your sides with palms upward (thumbs on top). Exhale — punch left hand to the front while looking forward with eyes wide open fiercely. Open left hand and rotate to push palm forward with thumb pointing down. Twist hand and fingers inward to make another fist with left hand while Inhaling. Exhale — pull left fist back to left side while punching right fist forward slowly. This completes one cycle. Repeat alternating sides. Total: 6 cycles. Return feet to shoulder width, stand tall.
↑ Inhale: pull fist back, twist fingers in ↓ Exhale: punch forward with fierce eyes 🫁 Liver + Gallbladder · Releases Qi stagnation ⚙ Element: Wood
8

Bouncing the Feet and Vibrating the Back

背后七顛百病消
Bèi Hòu Qī Diān Bǎi Bìng Xiāo
Inhale — raise arms to approximately shoulder height or slightly higher while keeping palms facing downward. At the same time, lift heels off the ground and balance on the balls of your feet. Exhale with a little extra force — allow heels to hit the floor while dropping arms beside you. Let the movement be loose and natural. This completes one cycle. Total: 6 cycles.
↑ Inhale: raise arms and heels simultaneously ↓ Exhale (forceful): heels drop, arms drop 🫁 All organs · Vibration settles Qi throughout body ⚙ Element: Water / All

🌿 Closing Position · 結束 · After Every Session

Inhale — raise arms to each side with palms upward until just in front of and over your head. Exhale — lower hands with palms downward until you reach the lower dantian level. Perform two more cycles (3 total). Finish with hands resting on lower dantian and eyes closed for at least 30 seconds. Breathe naturally and relax body and mind. Open eyes, relax arms at sides, return left foot next to right foot.

TCM Theory
Key TCM Terms · 主要術語
氣
Qi (Chi)
Vital energy or life force that flows through pathways (meridians) in the body. The fundamental substance underlying all TCM theory and the goal of Qigong cultivation.
丹田
Dantian
Energy center located approximately 2 inches below the navel. The home base for all Ba Duan Jin movements — hands return here between each movement.
精
Jing
Essence — one of the Three Treasures (三寶). Stored in the kidneys. Related to reproductive energy, constitutional strength, and longevity. Strengthened by Movement #6.
神
Shen
Spirit or mind — one of the Three Treasures. Housed in the Heart. Governs consciousness, emotions, and mental clarity. Calmed by Qigong practice.
三焦
San Jiao
Triple Warmer or Triple Burner — one of the 6 organ systems. Governs water metabolism and thermal regulation throughout the body. Targeted by Movement #1.
經絡
Meridians
Pathways through which Qi flows throughout the body. 12 primary meridians correspond to organ systems. Qigong promotes smooth Qi flow through all meridians.
陰陽
Yin & Yang
Opposing yet complementary and interdependent forces. Yin: cool, dark, feminine, passive, descending. Yang: warm, light, masculine, active, ascending. Balance is health.
衛氣
Wei Qi
Defensive or protective Qi that circulates on the body's surface and protects against external pathogenic factors. Strengthened by immune Qigong (Week 8).
君火
Heart Fire
Excess Fire in the Heart causing restlessness, anxiety, insomnia, and irritability. Movement #5 (Sway Head and Shake the Tail) specifically reduces Heart Fire.
易筋經
Yi Jin Jing
Muscle and Tendon Changing Classic — a more advanced Qigong system introduced in Week 9. Focuses on strengthening tendons, muscles, and internal organs through vigorous movements.

CLO 3 Adaptation

CLO 3: Adapt and teach Qi Gong exercises to patients with movement disabilities

The goal is to preserve the therapeutic intention of each movement while making it accessible. When adapting, always maintain: breath coordination, mental focus, and the essential action of the targeted organ/meridian system. Chair-based and standing modifications are both acceptable.

# Movement Condition / Limitation Adaptation / Modification
1Holding Up the HeavensShoulder injury / limited ROMReduce arm raise height; clasp hands at chest level only; skip overhead push entirely
1Holding Up the HeavensBalance impairment / fall riskPerform fully seated; same breath and hand movements apply
2Draw the BowKnee / hip osteoarthritisRemain standing tall — no horse stance; perform all arm and shoulder movements at standing height
2Draw the BowWheelchair userUpper body only; no stepping or stance; rotate torso in chair for added benefit
3Separating Heaven & EarthShoulder injuryReduce extension height; stop at shoulder level; do not force overhead reach
3Separating Heaven & EarthLow blood pressure / dizzinessSlow the movement significantly; pause at chest before separating; sit if dizzy
4Wise Owl Gazes BackwardsCervical disc herniationLimit head rotation to a comfortable range; never force; can perform arm rotation only without head turn
4Wise Owl Gazes BackwardsVertigo / BPPVAvoid or greatly reduce head rotation; perform arm external rotation only without head movement
5Sway Head & Shake TailKnee injury / painStand tall — no horse stance; perform torso rotation in standing position
5Sway Head & Shake TailLumbar disc herniationGreatly reduce torso twist range; never aggressively lean over thigh; keep spine supported
6Hold Feet / Strengthen KidneysLumbar disc diseaseSignificantly limit forward bend; bend at hips with knees generously bent; never fully fold
6Hold Feet / Strengthen KidneysHigh blood pressureAvoid full forward bend (head below heart); limit to 45-degree bend maximum
6Hold Feet / Strengthen KidneysOsteoporosisSkip full forward bend entirely; substitute gentle standing waist rotation or seated version
7Clench Fists & Gaze FiercelyHand / wrist arthritisSoften fist grip; partial fist is acceptable; do not fully clench to the point of pain
7Clench Fists & Gaze FiercelyGlaucoma / eye conditionAvoid fiercely widening eyes; keep eyes soft and relaxed; rest of movement proceeds normally
8Bouncing Feet / Vibrating BackOsteoporosis — CRITICALSKIP heel drop impact entirely. Replace with gentle heel raise and very slow controlled lowering — no forceful drop to floor
8Bouncing Feet / Vibrating BackHip / knee joint replacementSkip all impact; gentle heel raise and lower only; no dropping; or skip movement entirely
AllAll movementsWheelchair / full mobility limitationAll 8 movements can be adapted to seated performance; emphasize upper body, breath, and mental intention
AllAll movementsPregnancy (2nd / 3rd trimester)Avoid deep forward bends and extreme range of motion; slow all movements; prioritize comfort and breath
